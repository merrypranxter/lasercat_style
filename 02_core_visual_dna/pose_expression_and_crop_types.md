# Pose, Expression, and Crop Types

`laser_cat_style` depends heavily on cat selection.
Not just any cat image will do.

This file defines the most useful pose families, expression types, and crop behaviors for the style.

A huge amount of the humor comes from choosing the right cat image before anything else gets added.

---

# Core principle

The best cat poses and crops are:
- weird
- readable
- iconic
- awkward
- transformable
- funny before the effects are even applied

The style loves cats that already look slightly wrong.

---

# Pose families

## 1. Upright humanoid cat

The cat stands or appears to stand in a strangely vertical, person-like way.

Use cases:
- dancing cat scenes
- deity or witness mode
- glam accessory cat
- sticker-scatter compositions
- absurd group tableaux

Strengths:
- immediate weirdness
- graphic silhouette
- easy for scale manipulation
- works well in space or stage backgrounds

Risks:
- can become too "lol standing cat" if not escalated

---

## 2. Loaf or throne cat

The cat is compact, seated, stable, and visually self-important.

Use cases:
- sandwich cat
- pizza throne cat
- god-cat scenes
- object occupation gags
- central hero compositions

Strengths:
- reads like entitlement
- makes absurd vehicles or objects funnier
- stable central focal form

Risks:
- too passive if background and intensifiers are weak

---

## 3. Sprawl cat

The cat is stretched, flattened, or collapsed into a very object-compatible form.

Use cases:
- bread cat
- toilet paper cat
- floating food cat
- couch-like or raft-like compositions
- lazy cosmic drift scenes

Strengths:
- merges well with objects
- high dumbass potential
- useful for large-object jokes

Risks:
- can lose silhouette clarity

---

## 4. Mid-action cat

The cat is jumping, falling, lunging, swatting, or suspended mid-motion.

Use cases:
- attack scenes
- shark-riding or vehicle scenes
- object chaos
- laser emission action shots
- falling-through-space layouts

Strengths:
- kinetic energy
- strong drama
- excellent for single-gag hero mode

Risks:
- bad cutouts can destroy legibility if source quality is weak

---

## 5. Face-forward cat

The cat looks directly at camera.

Use cases:
- laser eye scenes
- psychedelic eye scenes
- giant face cat
- drug cat
- emotional confrontation gags

Strengths:
- direct eye contact
- easy focal center
- strong meme readability

Risks:
- without intensification, may become too plain

---

## 6. Side-look or diagonal-look cat

The cat glances off-axis or is caught in partial profile.

Use cases:
- witness cat
- weird collage tableau
- co-star scenes
- glam accessory mode
- floating in space while noticing bullshit

Strengths:
- gives the image an accidental narrative quality
- works well with scene intrusions

Risks:
- weaker for central deity mode unless scale is large

---

## 7. Screaming / open-mouth cat

The mouth is open in shock, scream, meow, hiss, or accidental goblin mode.

Use cases:
- rainbow puke
- psychedelic intensification
- mouth-emission scenes
- chemically wrecked cat imagery
- high-energy comedic closeups

Strengths:
- instant chaos
- perfect for emission-based gag logic
- emotionally loud

Risks:
- can become repetitive if every scene relies on open-mouth escalation

---

## 8. Tiny reaching or pawing cat

A smaller cat reaching upward or outward.

Use cases:
- UFO scenes
- floating object interaction
- tiny witness cat
- dramatic point-of-contact compositions

Strengths:
- excellent for relational comedy
- gives directionality to scenes

Risks:
- can be compositionally weak without larger support elements

# Expression families

## Blank idiot expression

One of the best possible expressions.
Emotionally unreadable but visually hilarious.

Use for:
- god-cat
- witness cat
- sandwich cat
- giant-cat city mode
- surreal authority

## Alarmed / wide-eyed expression

Useful for:
- food absurdity
- space displacement
- scene escalation
- co-star animal chaos

## Smug or serene expression

Useful for:
- throne cat
- glam trash cat
- deity cat
- "I belong here" object pairings

## Feral / battle expression

Useful for:
- laser cat
- destroyer cat
- action scenes
- shark riding
- city attack

## Screaming or chemically blasted expression

Useful for:
- drug-cat branch
- rainbow puke
- oversaturation scenes
- stoner meme overlap
- color-burn face hero images

# Crop types

## 1. Full-body crop

The entire cat is visible.

Best for:
- object riding
- scale jokes
- upright cat scenes
- composition readability
- scatter mode

## 2. Half-body crop

Useful when:
- the face matters
- one gesture matters
- accessory placement matters
- object fusion requires simplification

## 3. Big head crop

Massively useful for:
- laser eyes
- drug-cat branch
- giant face scenes
- confrontation compositions
- posterized color-burn cat

## 4. Face-only crop

Can be extremely strong when paired with:
- lasers
- glow
- psychedelic eyes
- rainbow mouth logic
- giant icon mode

Risk:
can become too simple unless supported by strong effects or context

## 5. Awkward accidental crop

A very internet-native option:
- slightly cut-off paws
- weird framing
- incomplete body
- crop that feels accidental or screenshot-ish

This can help preserve authenticity.

## 6. Overscaled crop

The cat is cropped too close or enlarged too much.

Useful for:
- giant-cat threat
- absurd intimacy
- monster-cat mode
- dumb hero emphasis

---

# Crop behaviors that help the style

## Readable wrongness
A crop can be weird as long as it still reads quickly.

## Source mismatch tolerance
Different crop scales and source qualities can coexist.

## Compression survival
The subject should remain funny even after image degradation.

## Sticker-compatibility
The crop should feel placeable like a movable collage piece.

---

# Useful pose-expression pairings

## Upright + blank
Great for cosmic witness cats and absurd group scenes.

## Loaf + smug
Excellent for pizza, sandwich, throne, and object-occupation jokes.

## Face-forward + intense eyes
Ideal for laser and psychedelic branches.

## Open mouth + close crop
Perfect for rainbow puke and altered-state cat.

## Mid-action + feral
Best for destroyer mode, vehicle riding, and attack scenes.

## Sprawl + confused
Excellent for object fusion, food merging, and lazy surreal drift.

---

# Bad crop and pose mistakes

## Overly tasteful pet photography crops
Too clean, too composed, too intentional.

## Tiny unreadable cat with no visual role
The cat disappears into the background and stops carrying the image.

## Complex pose with no clear silhouette
The cutout becomes a hairy blob.

## Emotionally neutral but compositionally dead pose
Not every blank face is useful. The pose still needs visual force.

## Perfect studio-cat posture with no corruption
Can drift toward commercial pet aesthetics unless aggressively broken.

---

# Selection rule of thumb

Choose cats that already look like:
- they don't belong where they are
- they might be powerful by accident
- they could survive being pasted onto something stupid
- they would become funnier if cut out badly

That is usually the correct subject pool.

---

# Bottom line

The best `laser_cat_style` poses and crops are not elegant.
They are:
- iconic
- transformable
- slightly busted
- emotionally legible
- compositionally resilient

The cat should already be halfway to a meme before the collage even starts.
