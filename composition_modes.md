# Composition Modes

`laser_cat_style` uses a limited but powerful set of recurring composition behaviors.

These are not just layout options.
They are joke-delivery systems.

This file defines the main composition modes that structure the style.

---

# Core principle

A strong `laser_cat_style` image is usually organized around one of two major modes:
1. **Sticker Scatter Mode**
2. **Single-Gag Hero Mode**

Additional secondary modes branch from those foundations:
3. Threat Tableau Mode
4. Object-Merge Mode
5. Group Chaos Mode
6. Graphic Poster Mode

Understanding these modes is essential for:
- prompt writing
- drift control
- scene generation
- example classification
- app system design

---

# 1. Sticker Scatter Mode

## Definition

Multiple discrete objects are placed across a field, often with weak physical relationship but strong visual joke energy.

Typical elements:
- one or more cats
- food items
- diamonds
- milkshake
- UFOs
- random objects
- rainbow emissions
- accessory bits

The scene behaves like a floating cloud of sticker elements.

## Characteristics
- modular placement
- shallow or fake depth
- object equality
- high nonsense density
- background acts as stage
- image reads like a curated pile of bullshit

## Best backgrounds
- galaxy fields
- black void stage
- bright gradients
- simple cosmic environments

## Tone
- maximalist
- playful
- dumb
- internety
- scattered but readable

## Strengths
- very style-faithful
- easy to vary procedurally
- supports object absurdity
- strongly meme-native

## Risks
- clutter
- focal collapse
- too many similarly sized elements
- random without hierarchy

---

# 2. Single-Gag Hero Mode

## Definition

One dominant image-premise carries the composition.

Examples:
- cat in bread
- cat on sandwich
- giant face cat with lasers
- cowboy cat riding shark
- pizza-riding hero cat

The image is about one joke first and everything else second.

## Characteristics
- one clear focal unit
- strong silhouette
- lower object count
- immediate "what the fuck" readability
- scene supports the main gag rather than competing with it

## Best backgrounds
- galaxy field
- black stage
- simple gradient
- dramatic contrast field

## Tone
- iconic
- concentrated
- stupid in a very direct way
- highly shareable

## Strengths
- strong readability
- strong meme power
- easy classification
- excellent for prompt systems

## Risks
- too simple
- under-escalated
- not enough support context
- drifting into novelty postcard blandness

---

# 3. Threat Tableau Mode

## Definition

A larger dramatic environment is established and the cat becomes an event inside it.

Examples:
- giant cat above city
- laser cat over skyline
- cat invasion beach scene
- cat as looming destructive presence

## Characteristics
- scene-based composition
- dramatic scale contrast
- environmental stakes
- cat as force rather than just object
- stronger narrative suggestion

## Best backgrounds
- city scenes
- beach chaos
- grayscale urban collage
- apocalypse cloud scenes

## Tone
- fake epic
- apocalyptic but funny
- majestic stupidity
- authority-cat absurdity

## Strengths
- visually memorable
- strong deity/destroyer energy
- good for monster/god-cat branch

## Risks
- drifting into serious kaiju art
- too cinematic
- too polished
- not enough cat idiot-energy

---

# 4. Object-Merge Mode

## Definition

The cat becomes directly fused with one dominant object.

Examples:
- cat in bread
- sandwich cat
- toilet paper cat
- object-wrapped cat
- cat-throne hybrid scenes

## Characteristics
- cat + object become one compositional unit
- very strong central read
- often deadpan
- highly effective for absurdity through literalness

## Tone
- blunt
- iconic
- low-explanation
- beautifully stupid

## Strengths
- highly repeatable
- strong for social media readability
- ideal for food and household branches

## Risks
- weak if object-cat relation is visually unclear
- can become generic novelty if the background is not supportive

---

# 5. Group Chaos Mode

## Definition

Multiple cats or multiple similar elements populate the scene, creating swarm energy or chorus behavior.

Examples:
- several laser cats attacking together
- dancing cat group in space
- multiple cats each on different pizzas
- cat armies or distributed cat-cloud chaos

## Characteristics
- repeated forms
- chorus-like absurdity
- distributed attention
- stronger collective weirdness than single-icon clarity

## Tone
- celebratory
- dumbly theatrical
- chaotic
- meme-wallpaper adjacent

## Strengths
- high-energy
- strong maximalist appeal
- works well with repeated motif families

## Risks
- too noisy
- loss of hierarchy
- visual flattening if all cats are equal in scale and importance

---

# 6. Graphic Poster Mode

## Definition

A more reduced, contrast-driven, stylized version of the style with fewer elements and stronger formal composition.

Examples:
- black cat silhouette over grayscale city with rainbow beams
- cutout cat + one or two large graphical inserts
- lower-clutter collage with bold focal contrast

## Characteristics
- stronger design control
- fewer objects
- more graphic silhouette
- less junk density
- higher contrast between serious framing and stupid subject logic

## Tone
- cooler
- more poster-like
- more Tumblr-adjacent
- still absurd, but less spammy

## Strengths
- elegant edge branch
- useful allowed variant
- strong readability
- good for print or poster adaptation

## Risks
- becoming too tasteful
- drifting into general surreal collage
- losing tacky meme energy

---

# Composition hierarchy rules

No matter the mode, the scene usually needs:

## 1. Primary focal read
What do you see first?

Usually:
- the cat
- the cat-object hybrid
- the cat's eyes or mouth
- the giant-cat silhouette

## 2. Secondary support read
What explains or intensifies the joke?

Usually:
- food
- glow
- lasers
- UFO
- city
- accessory
- co-star animal

## 3. Background field
What emotional or tonal stage holds the event?

Usually:
- galaxy
- gradient
- void
- apocalypse scene
- grayscale collage world

Without hierarchy, the image becomes sludge.

---

# Scale distribution by mode

## Sticker Scatter
Mix large, medium, and small objects.
Never let everything sit at one size.

## Hero Mode
One dominant unit, minimal but useful support.

## Threat Tableau
Huge cat, medium environment, tiny humans/buildings or vulnerable scene components.

## Group Chaos
At least one dominant cat or cluster anchor, or the swarm gets mushy.

## Graphic Poster
One to three strong scales only. Keep it crisp.

---

# Choosing the right mode

Use this test:

## Choose Sticker Scatter if:
- the joy comes from many random inserts
- the object cloud matters
- the image should feel like a desktop of cursed PNGs

## Choose Single-Gag Hero if:
- the joke is one clear absurd pairing
- silhouette clarity is the main strength
- the image needs instant readability

## Choose Threat Tableau if:
- the cat should feel powerful, destructive, or divine
- the scene needs environmental stakes

## Choose Object-Merge if:
- one object-cat fusion is the whole joke

## Choose Group Chaos if:
- repetition is funny
- cat chorus energy matters

## Choose Graphic Poster if:
- the image should sit on the cooler, cleaner edge of the style while preserving core DNA

---

# Anti-drift composition warnings

Avoid:
- cinematic balance that feels too refined
- too much empty tasteful negative space
- symmetrical poster design unless used ironically
- composition so cluttered the cat is no longer central
- physical realism overriding placement humor
- centerless chaos with no main read

`laser_cat_style` can be messy.
It cannot be compositionally dead.

---

# Bottom line

Composition in `laser_cat_style` is the delivery mechanism for stupidity.

The two most important engines are:
- **Sticker Scatter Mode**
- **Single-Gag Hero Mode**

Everything else branches from those.

A good image feels random at first glance,
but underneath it, the composition is doing very specific work.
