# Style Definition

`laser_cat_style` is an internet-native visual collage style built around **cats as absurd central agents** within bright, cheap, surreal, tacky, or cosmic compositions.

The style usually combines:
- cat cutouts
- visibly artificial compositing
- bright synthetic or high-contrast backgrounds
- a deliberately stupid premise
- one or more visual intensifiers such as lasers, rainbows, food, UFOs, glam objects, or scale distortion

At its core, the style is not about realism, polish, or coherent worldbuilding.

It is about:
- **juxtaposition**
- **color violence**
- **object nonsense**
- **collage wrongness**
- **cat-centered absurdity**

---

# Short definition

A usable short definition:

> `laser_cat_style` is an early-internet cat collage aesthetic in which badly or semi-badly composited cats are placed in ridiculous cosmic, meme-like, or surreal situations and intensified with lasers, rainbows, food, objects, or tacky visual effects.

---

# Core identity

The style depends on five things:

## 1. Cat centrality
A cat is not a minor accessory.
The cat must be a major visual driver:
- focal point
- power source
- victim
- idiot
- deity
- rider
- projectile
- witness
- object inhabitant

Without true cat centrality, the image drifts into generic surreal meme collage.

## 2. Collage obviousness
The image should feel composited.
Even when technically better made, it should preserve some awareness of cutout logic, source mismatch, or sticker-like assembly.
Invisible polish weakens the style.

## 3. Absurd premise
The scene must operate on a stupid or delightfully unreasonable idea:
- cat riding pizza
- giant cat over city
- rainbow eyes destroying skyline
- cat inside bread
- cat floating with fries and milkshake
- cat with acid tab in a cosmic glam void

The absurdity should be immediate, not buried.

## 4. Synthetic intensification
The style loves added visual escalation:
- rainbow emissions
- lasers
- neon glows
- galaxy backdrops
- floating junk
- costume elements
- glittery trash
- posterized colors
- hallucinatory eyes

The image should usually feel "pushed."

## 5. Correct badness
A crucial part of the style is that it often looks:
- cheap
- crusty
- cut out
- low-res
- glow-bloated
- awkwardly layered
- visually incorrect in a charming way

That does **not** mean any random bad image qualifies.
The badness must support the joke and the visual identity.

---

# Expanded definition

`laser_cat_style` belongs to a broader family of online image aesthetics where humor, collage, and visual trashiness combine into a recognizable visual language.

Its distinguishing features are:
- cat-focused composition
- meme-native surrealism
- bright or dramatic visual escalation
- obvious or semi-obvious digital compositing
- random but recurring object families
- tonal oscillation between cute and deranged
- a low respect for realism
- a high respect for the joke

It is often adjacent to:
- early Tumblr collage
- MySpace-era rainbow internet graphics
- stoner meme aesthetics
- cosmic animal meme art
- low-budget digital surrealism
- novelty poster design
- internet sticker-cloud compositions

But it has its own identity because the **cat**, the **collage**, and the **escalation gimmick** are all structurally central.

---

# Defining ingredients

## Primary ingredients
Most successful examples contain at least three of these, and often more:
- cat cutout
- galaxy / nebula / gradient / void background
- laser eyes or rainbow emission
- food intrusion
- random floating object
- tacky accessory
- scale absurdity
- glow or halo
- low-res or crusty texture
- visually stupid but readable gag

## Optional intensifiers
These often strengthen the style:
- UFOs
- diamonds
- sunglasses
- hats
- other animals
- apocalypse setting
- grayscale city with rainbow contrast
- psychedelic eye patterns
- drugs or stoner references
- object swarm scatter

---

# Formal visual logic

## Subject logic
The cat must feel iconic, not incidental.

## Gag logic
There should be an instantly legible "what the fuck" premise.

## Background logic
The background usually provides drama or tackiness rather than realism.

## Artifact logic
Edges, compression, glow, masking, and source mismatch are often preserved rather than corrected.

## Tone logic
The image should feel unserious, campy, or absurd even when it looks weirdly majestic.

---

# Emotional register

The style tends to produce one or more of the following reactions:
- laughter
- dumb delight
- affectionate disbelief
- internet nostalgia
- "this is so stupid"
- "why does this kind of rule though"
- low-grade psychedelic amusement
- deranged cuteness

It is not usually aiming for awe in the traditional fine-art sense, even when it borrows cosmic imagery.

Its awe is usually **stupid awe**.

---

# Style sentence

A one-sentence identity statement:

> `laser_cat_style` turns cats into brightly composited absurdist icons by placing them inside tacky, cosmic, food-filled, or destructive collage scenarios and preserving the delicious wrongness of internet-era digital image making.

---

# Necessary conditions

An image usually needs all of the following to strongly qualify:
1. a cat is central
2. the image feels composited or collage-built
3. the premise is absurd or memetically escalated
4. the visual language is intensified beyond ordinary pet photography
5. the result preserves some degree of intentional incorrectness or tacky excess

If one or more of those are missing, the image may be adjacent but not core.

---

# Exclusions

An image does **not** strongly belong if it is merely:
- a normal cat with a galaxy overlay
- a polished digital fantasy cat portrait
- a cat with one meme element pasted in but no broader style logic
- a pastel cute cat illustration with no collage energy
- a "trippy" cat image with no joke structure
- a slick poster that smooths away all the crust

---

# Bottom line

`laser_cat_style` is a style of **cat-powered digital absurdity**.

It is held together by:
- cat centrality
- collage visibility
- stupid premise clarity
- tacky or cosmic escalation
- correct badness

Lose those, and the style falls apart.
