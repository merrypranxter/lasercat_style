# Glow, Blur, and Aura Behavior

Glow, blur, and aura effects are essential artifacts in `laser_cat_style`.

## Glow types

### Edge glow
- Outline around cat or objects
- Neon color
- Sharp inner, soft outer

### Full glow
- Entire subject emanates light
- Aura-like
- High intensity

### Point glow
- Specific points emit light
- Eyes, mouth, accessories
- Beam source

### Environmental glow
- Light affects surroundings
- Cast glow on background
- Atmospheric

## Blur types

### Motion blur
- Suggests movement
- Action scenes
- Dynamic energy

### Glow blur
- Softens light edges
- Creates aura
- Reduces harshness

### Depth blur
- Fake depth of field
- Foreground/background separation
- Not always style-native

## Aura behavior

### Cat aura
- Surrounds entire cat
- Color-coded to effect
- Spiritual/cosmic suggestion

### Object aura
- Surrounds specific objects
- Highlights importance
- Adds visual interest

### Beam aura
- Surrounds laser or emission
- Intensifies effect
- Adds power

## Best practices
- Glow should be obvious, not subtle
- Blur should serve the effect
- Aura should enhance, not obscure
- Effects should be tacky, not elegant

## Drift risks
- Too subtle (loses impact)
- Too elegant (becomes design)
- Obscures cat (loses subject)
- Realistic lighting (wrong aesthetic)
