# JPEG Crust, Noise, and Resolution

JPEG artifacts, noise, and resolution issues are part of the style's internet-native texture.

## JPEG crust

### What it is
- Compression artifacts
- Blocky patterns
- Color banding
- Edge degradation

### Why it matters
- Signals repost culture
- Internet-native authenticity
- Visual history
- Meme heritage

### How to use
- Preserve in source images
- Simulate for authenticity
- Don't overdo (unreadable)
- Balance with clarity

## Noise types

### Compression noise
- JPEG artifact patterns
- Block boundaries
- Color quantization

### Grain
- Film-like texture
- Added character
- Vintage feel

### Digital noise
- Sensor artifacts
- Low-light simulation
- Texture addition

## Resolution behavior

### Low-res charm
- Pixelation
- Softness
- Thumbnail energy
- Repost survival

### High-res risk
- Too clean
- Loses crust
- Can drift to polish

### Resolution rules
- Readable at target size
- Some softness acceptable
- Avoid excessive pixelation
- Balance clarity with character

## Best practices
- Some crust adds authenticity
- Don't clean too much
- Preserve internet texture
- Maintain readability

## Drift risks
- Too clean (loses authenticity)
- Too crusty (unreadable)
- Over-restored (polished drift)
- Artificially aged (fake)
