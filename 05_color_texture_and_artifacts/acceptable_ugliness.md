# Acceptable Ugliness

`laser_cat_style` embraces a specific type of "badness" as part of its aesthetic.

## Core principle

Not all ugliness is equal.
The style wants **correct ugliness**, not random badness.

## Acceptable ugly

### Cutout roughness
- Visible edges
- Imperfect masking
- Sticker energy

### Glow excess
- Overbright
- Blown highlights
- Tacky intensity

### Color clipping
- Saturated beyond natural
- Synthetic intensity
- Neon violence

### Compression artifacts
- JPEG crust
- Blocky patterns
- Internet texture

### Source mismatch
- Different lighting
- Different resolution
- Obvious compositing

### Scale wrongness
- Obviously wrong proportions
- Absurd relationships
- Meme logic

## Unacceptable ugly

### Unreadable
- Cat lost
- Silhouette destroyed
- Visual mush

### Unintentional
- Accidental mistakes
- No aesthetic purpose
- Random badness

### Traumatic
- Genuinely disturbing
- Body horror
- Unpleasant content

### Boring
- No escalation
- No joke
- No visual interest

## The ugliness spectrum

```
Polished (drift) → Acceptable ugly (core) → Unreadable (failure)
```

The goal is the middle zone:
- Rough enough to be authentic
- Clear enough to be readable
- Tacky enough to be funny
- Crusty enough to be internet-native

## Best practices
- Preserve some roughness
- Don't over-clean
- Embrace tackiness
- Maintain readability
- Avoid perfection

## Drift risks
- Too polished (loses authenticity)
- Too ugly (unreadable)
- Wrong type of ugly (not style-native)
