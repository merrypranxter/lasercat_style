# Cutout Edges and Masking Halos

Cutout edges and masking halos are key artifacts that signal collage logic.

## Cutout edge types

### Hard edge
- Sharp, clean cut
- Obvious digital selection
- Sticker-like

### Soft edge
- Feathered selection
- Slight blur
- Gentler transition

### Fuzzy edge
- Rough selection
- Fur detail preserved
- Natural but obvious

### Crunchy edge
- Compression artifacts
- Pixelation
- Internet-trash energy

## Halo types

### White halo
- Light fringe around subject
- Classic cutout artifact
- Immediately signals collage

### Color halo
- Colored fringe
- Glow-like
- Effect-enhanced

### Dark halo
- Shadow fringe
- Depth suggestion
- Less common

## Best practices
- Some edge visibility is good
- Halo can be feature not bug
- Roughness adds authenticity
- Perfect edges can drift toward polish

## Drift risks
- Invisible edges (seamless realism)
- No halo (too clean)
- Perfect masking (too polished)
- Edge contamination (unreadable)
