# Laser Eyes

Laser eyes are one of the most iconic motifs in `laser_cat_style`.
They are not merely an effect.
They are a role assignment, a humor engine, a composition tool, and a shortcut to false grandeur.

This file defines how laser eyes function in the style.

---

# Core principle

Laser eyes transform a cat from:
- subject
into
- source

They make the cat:
- powerful
- destructive
- fake-divine
- absurdly consequential
- instantly memetic

Laser eyes are especially useful because they combine:
- cat centrality
- escalation
- visual directionality
- joke clarity
- cheap digital spectacle

---

# Why laser eyes work so well

Laser eyes are powerful in this style because they do several jobs at once.

## 1. They assign power
A blank-faced cat with lasers is instantly upgraded into a force.

## 2. They create composition lines
The beams guide the eye and structure the scene.

## 3. They create false grandeur
The cat appears weirdly godlike or apocalyptic.

## 4. They preserve stupidity
Laser eyes are a deeply lowbrow, meme-native absurdity.
Even when the image looks dramatic, the effect remains gloriously dumb.

## 5. They tolerate cheap execution
Lasers can still work when:
- glows are crude
- edges are rough
- effects are too bright
- beam geometry is physically ridiculous

This makes them perfect for the style.

---

# Basic laser-eye formula

A strong laser-eye image usually contains:
- cat face or forward-facing cat
- readable eyes
- one or two strong beam paths
- a target zone or implied target
- optional glow or environmental reaction
- a cat expression that does not fully deserve this level of power

That last part matters.

---

# Major laser-eye modes

## 1. Straight beam mode

Classic beam projection from both eyes outward in roughly linear paths.

Best for:
- city destruction
- cosmic confrontation
- simple hero scenes
- graphic poster mode

Strengths:
- immediate
- iconic
- easy to read
- highly memetic

Risks:
- becoming generic meme-laser art if not anchored in stronger style logic

---

## 2. Divergent beam mode

The beams spread or aim in different directions.

Best for:
- chaos scenes
- object-cloud interaction
- over-escalated compositions
- sticker scatter intensification

Strengths:
- more energetic
- more absurdly destructive
- more compositional agitation

Risks:
- clutter or directional confusion

---

## 3. Cone or blast mode

The eye emission is broad, exaggerated, or more like a giant field of force than a tight beam.

Best for:
- psychedelic overlap
- giant-cat scenes
- theatrical beam judgment
- graphic exaggeration

Strengths:
- high intensity
- fake-apocalyptic energy
- more stupidly overpowered

Risks:
- eyes may lose definition if cone is too broad

---

## 4. Rainbow laser mode

The laser uses rainbow or prismatic coloration instead of a simple red sci-fi beam.

Best for:
- crossover with rainbow-emission branches
- cosmic glam cat
- tacky maximalism
- softer but still absurd destruction

Strengths:
- highly style-faithful
- combines attack + tacky rainbow logic
- excellent for deity/idiot contradiction

Risks:
- can drift toward generic rainbow-cat art if scene logic is weak

---

## 5. Glow-heavy pseudo-laser mode

The emission is more smeared, glowy, or aura-like than sharply beam-defined.

Best for:
- cheap edit energy
- more crusty meme variants
- psychedelic closeups
- loose hero compositions

Strengths:
- low technical barrier
- strong internet-trash charm
- compatible with rough source material

Risks:
- effect may become too vague if not directional enough

---

# Cat types best suited for laser eyes

Laser-eye scenes work best with cats that are:
- face-forward
- blankly intense
- wide-eyed
- smugly calm
- awkwardly serious
- large in frame
- readable at thumbnail size

Excellent candidates:
- giant face crops
- neutral loaf faces
- alert forward-facing cats
- unintentionally threatening closeups

Less useful:
- cat poses where eyes are tiny or obscured
- heavily side-facing cats unless the composition clearly supports directional beam travel

---

# Tonal behavior

Laser eyes often push the image toward:
- destroyer cat
- deity cat
- fake judgment scene
- apocalyptic camp
- chemically intensified nonsense
- overconfident power fantasy

The best laser-eye images preserve a contradiction:
the cat looks too dumb, too calm, or too loaf-like to deserve the beams.

That contradiction is a huge part of the joke.

---

# Common targets and environments

Laser eyes become stronger when they interact with something.

Common targets:
- cities
- skylines
- random floating junk
- burger clouds
- UFOs
- empty void
- atmospheric horizon
- viewer-facing space

The target does not need detailed realism.
It only needs enough consequence to give the beam meaning.

---

# Color behavior

## Classic red laser
- strongest meme shorthand
- more aggressive
- more sci-fi coded

## Rainbow laser
- more tacky
- more style-native
- better for glam / psychedelic overlap

## Neon pink / cyan / magenta beam
- strong for cosmic-field scenes
- good for graphic poster variants
- more internet-artificial

## White-hot or multicolor beam
- stronger fake divinity
- stronger visual overload
- useful in high-escalation scenes

The beam should usually contrast well with:
- cat face
- background field
- target zone

---

# Laser-eye composition strategies

## Hero focus
Large cat face, clear beams, minimal clutter.

## Threat tableau
Cat dominates city or environment, beams assign fake stakes.

## Scatter intensifier
Cat exists within an object cloud while beams add aggression or directional structure.

## Poster confrontation
Graphic cat silhouette or face with bold beam direction and simplified field.

---

# Good cheapness vs bad cheapness

## Good cheapness
- slightly blown-out glow
- oversimplified beam shape
- obvious compositing
- bright color clipping
- cartoonishly unrealistic beam path

## Bad cheapness
- beam obscures the cat's face entirely
- beam edges so sloppy they become unreadable
- colors too muddy to register
- beam added without relation to composition

Cheap is welcome.
Unreadable is not.

---

# Common failure modes

## Generic laser-eye meme drift
A cat with beams, but no broader style engine.

Fix:
- add branch identity
- add stronger background or object logic
- reassert tone contradiction

## Serious sci-fi drift
The beam becomes too cinematic, too believable, too cool.

Fix:
- reintroduce tackiness
- blunt the realism
- make the cat dumber or calmer
- add lowbrow absurd context

## Effect dominance drift
The beam becomes more important than the cat.

Fix:
- improve eye readability
- enlarge cat face
- reduce beam clutter
- simplify target zone

## Under-escalation
Tiny beam, weak scene, no drama.

Fix:
- increase intensity
- increase target stakes
- increase scale contrast
- add fake-apocalyptic framing

# Useful branch overlaps

Laser eyes combine especially well with:
- monster / god cat
- space collage cat
- graphic collage cat
- glam trash cat
- psychedelic eyes cat
- city apocalypse backgrounds

They combine less well with:
- very soft kawaii variants
- underdeveloped food scenes unless used as comedic overkill

---

# Prompting ideas

Useful structural prompt ideas include:
- blank-faced cat with enormous laser eyes over grayscale city
- loaf cat seated on absurd object while rainbow beams slice through space
- giant cat face hovering above skyline, cheap neon beam glow, tacky cosmic field
- wide-eyed cat as false apocalyptic judge with overbright meme lasers

The trick is not just "laser eyes."
The trick is:

**laser eyes on a cat who has no business wielding that much power**

---

# Bottom line

Laser eyes are one of the style's most efficient motifs because they instantly provide:
- power
- comedy
- direction
- escalation
- false grandeur
- meme readability

A good laser-eye cat should feel like:
- a cosmic judge
- a fake god
- a destructive icon
- and still, somehow, a complete idiot
