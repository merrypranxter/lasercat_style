# UFO and Sci-Fi Intrusions

UFOs and sci-fi intrusions are recurring motifs in `laser_cat_style` that add:
- cosmic context
- absurd threat or contact
- retro-futurist tackiness
- visual interest
- narrative suggestion
- scale contrast

This file defines how these elements function in the style.

---

# Core principle

UFOs and sci-fi objects are not meant to be realistic.
They are meant to be:
- iconic
- meme-legible
- slightly retro or cartoonish
- absurdly placed
- compositionally useful
- tonally funny

The UFO should feel like a sticker or clipart insert, not a cinematic effect.

---

# Why UFOs work

UFOs are powerful in this style because they:
- instantly signal "space"
- are visually simple
- work at many scales
- add narrative without requiring explanation
- contrast well with cats
- feel internet-native
- can be tacky without losing function

A cat + UFO scene is immediately readable as:
- cosmic contact
- absurd abduction
- sci-fi nonsense
- meme tableau

---

# UFO types

## 1. Classic flying saucer

The most iconic form.

Characteristics:
- disc shape
- dome on top
- lights or glow
- retro sci-fi aesthetic
- cartoon or clipart energy

Best for:
- most core examples
- cosmic collage cat
- contact scenes
- abduction gags

---

## 2. Modern sci-fi craft

More angular, detailed, or contemporary designs.

Characteristics:
- sharper edges
- more panel detail
- less retro charm
- more action-movie energy

Use with caution:
- can drift toward serious sci-fi illustration
- may lose the style's tacky meme energy

---

## 3. Glowing orb or light

Simpler, more abstract cosmic presence.

Characteristics:
- sphere or light blob
- aura or glow
- less defined shape
- more mystical or energy-coded

Best for:
- psychedelic overlap
- drug-cat branch
- more abstract cosmic scenes

---

## 4. Multiple UFO swarm

Several craft in formation or scattered.

Characteristics:
- repetition
- pattern or chaos
- scale variety
- increased cosmic density

Best for:
- sticker scatter mode
- invasion or contact scenes
- high-escalation compositions

---

# UFO-cat relationship types

## Contact
The cat and UFO are in direct visual or implied narrative relation.

Examples:
- cat looking at UFO
- UFO beam on cat
- cat reaching toward UFO
- cat being abducted

## Coexistence
The cat and UFO share space without direct interaction.

Examples:
- cat in foreground, UFO in background
- cat and UFO as equal floating objects
- cosmic scene with both present

## Threat
The UFO implies danger or invasion toward the cat or scene.

Examples:
- multiple UFOs approaching
- beam targeting cat
- ominous positioning

## Absurd tableau
The UFO is just one more ridiculous element in a scatter composition.

Examples:
- cat + UFO + fries + diamond + milkshake
- object cloud with UFO as member

---

# Beam behavior

UFO beams are a major visual tool.

## Abduction beam
Downward cone or cylinder of light.

Use for:
- contact gags
- cat-in-peril or cat-as-target humor
- dramatic composition lines

## Spotlight beam
Simple downward light.

Use for:
- highlighting the cat
- adding glow without full abduction narrative

## Threat beam
Beam aimed at cat or environment.

Use for:
- fake danger
- dramatic escalation
- destroyer-cat counterpoint

## Decorative glow
Ambient light around the UFO without clear directional beam.

Use for:
- adding cosmic atmosphere
- supporting the sci-fi mood
- less narrative, more visual

---

# Scale behaviors

## UFO larger than cat
Creates threat, contact, or dramatic counterpoint.

## UFO equal to cat
Creates peer relationship or object-cloud equality.

## UFO smaller than cat
Creates absurd scale inversion, often funny.

## Multiple sizes
Creates depth, pattern, or scatter richness.

# Common failure modes

## Too realistic
The UFO looks like a cinematic effect.

Fix:
- make it more clipart
- add retro styling
- reduce detail
- increase tackiness

## Too dominant
The UFO overpowers the cat.

Fix:
- reduce UFO size
- move UFO to background
- increase cat presence
- simplify UFO detail

## Generic sci-fi drift
The image becomes serious UFO art with no cat-stupid humor.

Fix:
- add cat idiocy
- add absurd context
- add food or household objects
- reduce cinematic drama

## Under-integrated
The UFO looks pasted on without relation to the scene.

Fix:
- add beam or glow interaction
- position for compositional flow
- add narrative suggestion
- make the UFO part of the object cloud
