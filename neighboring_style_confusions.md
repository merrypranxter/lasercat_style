# Neighboring Style Confusions

`laser_cat_style` sits near several adjacent visual styles.

This file explains how to distinguish the style from its neighbors and prevent confusion.

---

# Core principle

Adjacent styles share some traits but differ in core identity.

Confusion with neighbors is a major source of drift.

Understanding the differences helps:
- classify examples correctly
- build accurate prompts
- prevent style collapse
- maintain identity

---

# Prevention strategies

## Strategy 1: Checklist comparison
Run images through neighbor comparison checklists.

## Strategy 2: Motif verification
Ensure style-native motifs are present.

## Strategy 3: Tone audit
Verify absurd, campy, stupid tone.

## Strategy 4: Composition confirmation
Confirm sticker scatter or single-gag hero mode.

## Strategy 5: Cat centrality test
Verify the cat is specifically central and specifically stupid.

# Bottom line

Neighboring styles are real and overlapping.

Confusion with neighbors causes drift.

Prevention requires:
- awareness of neighbors
- understanding of differences
- systematic comparison
- consistent verification

The style is distinct.
Keep it that way.
