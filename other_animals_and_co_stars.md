# Other Animals and Co-Stars

Other animals can appear in `laser_cat_style` as:
- co-riders
- mounts
- witnesses
- scene partners
- absurd companions
- chaos agents

This file defines how other animals function in the style.

---

# Core principle

Other animals are not replacements for the cat.
They are:
- scene enhancers
- narrative partners
- absurdity amplifiers
- relational comedy

The cat should remain central even when other animals are present.

---

# Why other animals work

Other animals add:
- narrative possibility
- scale contrast
- species absurdity
- relational humor
- scene richness
- meme-native recognition

A cat riding a shark is iconic.
A cat with an alpaca is absurd.
A cat and dog in cosmic chaos is funny.

# Co-star relationship types

## Mount
The other animal carries the cat.

Examples:
- cat riding shark
- cat on unicorn
- cat on alpaca

## Partner
The animals share the scene as equals.

Examples:
- cat and dog in space
- cat and bird tableau

## Witness
The other animal observes the cat's absurdity.

Examples:
- dog watching cat
- bird near cat chaos

## Chaos agent
The other animal adds to the nonsense.

Examples:
- multiple animals in scatter
- animal cloud scenes

---

# Scale behaviors

## Co-star larger than cat
Creates mount or dramatic contrast.

## Co-star equal to cat
Creates partnership or peer relationship.

## Co-star smaller than cat
Creates witness or accent role.

## Multiple sizes
Creates visual hierarchy and scene richness.

# Common failure modes

## Co-star dominance
The other animal overpowers the cat.

Fix:
- reduce co-star size
- move co-star to background
- increase cat presence
- simplify co-star detail

## Generic animal scene
The image is just "animals together" with no absurdity.

Fix:
- add cosmic background
- add absurd context
- add object scatter
- make the relationship ridiculous

## Serious wildlife drift
The image becomes nature art with no cat-stupid humor.

Fix:
- add cat idiocy
- add absurd context
- add cosmic background
- reduce naturalism

## Under-integrated
The co-star looks pasted on without relation to the scene.

Fix:
- add interaction
- position for compositional flow
- add narrative suggestion
- make the co-star part of the object cloud
